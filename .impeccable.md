## Design Context

### Users
Recruiters, potential freelance/contract clients, and the developer/peer community — all evaluating whether to interview, hire, or collaborate. The portfolio must quickly convey technical depth, shipped work, and a distinctive point of view.

### Brand Personality
**Raw, experimental, edgy.** Not buttoned-up corporate. Not generic "clean and modern." The WebGL shader, dark theme, and monospace labeling set the tone — this is someone who builds things that feel alive, not templated.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Theme**: Dark-only for now (light mode is a future consideration, not a current gap).
- **Anti-references**: Generic dev portfolio templates — card grids with icon+heading+text, gradient text, glassmorphism, cyan/purple AI palettes, Inter/Roboto system fonts. Anything that looks AI-generated.
- **References**: The WoeHeatmap shader in the hero is the anchor — it's custom WebGL, reactive to container size, and unlike any template. The aesthetic should extend from that DNA.

### Design Principles
1. **Custom over template** — Every visual element should feel authored, not assembled from a UI kit. The shader, the dot-matrix loader, the TracingBeam timeline — these are bespoke.
2. **Dark as atmosphere, not default** — The darkness isn't a shortcut; it creates a stage for the accent red and the shader to land.
3. **Mono precision** — Monospace labels, tight tracking, and technical typography signal engineering discipline. No decorative fluff.
4. **Work first, personality second** — The content (experience, projects, skills) is structured and scannable. Personality comes from details (the shader, the loader, the beam), not from layout gimmicks.
5. **Performance as craft** — The shader is WebGL, the loader is a dot matrix, the beam uses spring physics. Performance isn't just fast — it's part of the aesthetic.
